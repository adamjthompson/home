10 Best Sites

Picked just for you, these ten sites are sure to give a designer

inspiration for their own site! Below are a list of websites

with many different design styles in build, navigation, and

other unique characteristics sure to inspire designs for your

own site!

We hope these sites will help, and if you have any suggustions

for next weeks ten best sites, contact us down below! Thank

you for following up with our week's top ten!

 

Site Goal(s): Plume is an adventure video game made in WebGL for Christmas Experiments 2018 opening. The site would most likely want people to play the game, like it, and help bring others to play it as well.

Target Audience: The audience would be gamers, artists, possibly youtube gaming channels, and kids because the game doesn’t appear violent. This game would be for those who like puzzle, adventure, and story games. Also for those who like Christmas.

Navigation:The navigation is very straight on. You are first asked what screen quality you want and then you are lead to the main screen above and just hit go to play.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use):The main menu has it where the mouse pulls your view around, which is unique and when you hover over parts in the menu, it pulls your mouse in, giving you a sign that you can click here.

Content:The story cut scenes are straight to the point in telling you your mission as the character. The basic keyboard controls are w,s,a,and,d to move. But the rest is for you to discover.

Design/Layout: The layout is very detailed, but they use a very monochromatic grey. The theme of this game is Christmas, which is supposed to be a jolly holiday. But the reason it is gloomy is because the main character was forgotten by Santa, so the gloomy and dull atmosphere.

https://plumegame.com/

 

Site Goal(s): The goal of this site is to display a weird little story. I think it is meant to make people laugh and enjoy the little display they created.

Target Audience: I would say the target audience is a little older than children, but not that children can’t use. The inside of the cabin leads me to believe this is a joke about people who take drugs.
Navigation: Fairly simple.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use):The use of the site is very smooth when it comes to going to where you need to go. Simple directions pop up, such as click and drag or click and hold. Nothing to complicated to understand.

Content: Content is not really there. You have one simple designated point and that is it. You are lead to the cabin and can click out off cabin but that is it. That was a disappointment because I felt they could have added more.

Design/Layout: Other than the content to be a tad bit disappointing, the outside and surrounding area of the cabin is very beautiful. The use of complimentary colors yellow and purple are very astounding and very unique as an art style. What for me ruined it was the inside of the cabin with the elf. However, the elf can be seen as an element of its own. For instance, the viewer goes from this soft and pretty art style to this extremely colorful and trippy area. But I took note of this because I liked how the beginning was set and the use of color

for inspiration.

http://littlehelper.resn.global/

Site Goal(s): The sites goals is to showcase artists work and their visual interpretation of others albums.

Target Audience: The target audience would be artist, those who like primary colors, and album lovers.

Navigation: At first I wasn’t sure what to do, but you have to scroll down for everything to appear. As you go down, information slides onto the screen.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): Finding where to go and scrolling for the links is very easy. The links light up when you hover over them so you know where to go. They are also very descriptive so you know if that’s where you want to go next.

Content: The albums are all labeled so you know who created them and they are displayed so no one else’s design takes up too much space. They have many different style show cased, so there is room for all kinds of inspiration.

Design/Layout: The color theme is my personal favorite, primary colors. Everything is well balanced in separate sections, but nothing is too different. You can tell that each section belongs together. But at the same time, there are different designs that are united by the same theme. This could be a way to organize a web site.

http://10x17.co/

 

Site Goal(s): I think the purpose of the site is more of a metaphor. That people have animalistic traits in their actions and the art

shows this.

Target Audience: Young adults, adults, artists, and those who want a good laugh.

Navigation: The navigation is very simple, no clicking. You just scroll down and everything starts to slide in and display itself.

Content: Content is there, but it is short and sweet. But when you think about it, most people don’t like to read that much. This page is also not super serious either, since what is said isn’t major or important, but just a lot of pun like quotes.
Design/Layout: The color theme is mostly complimentary blues and oranges. Some content stays put as you scroll, which is a nice touch. Everything is simple and the font is easy to read and gets to the point.

http://zoomanity.land/en/

 

Site Goal(s): To sell and bring back polaroid cameras, new cameras, and accessories.

Target Audience: Photographers, graphic designers, vintage item lovers, historians, and adults who grew up with polaroid cameras.

Navigation:Traveling within the website is very smooth. Everything loads rather quickly and categories are labeled in such a way so you can find what your looking for. Nothing is too busy or complicated.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use):The different links are easy to access to go to where you want to go. They are a simple layout at the top, but you can still find what you need. The images also turn when you hover so you get a better look at the items you might want to purchase.

Content:The items are labeled of what they are and how much they are for. The images also help when searching for what you want. They have an different categories of time periods of items too in case you want a specific product.

Design/Layout: The design is very straight forward. The site uses a great deal of photography of the products, so you know exactly what they are trying to sell. The font used is easy to read and when you hover over a link, the other links falter a bit so the one you are on stands out.

https://eu.polaroidoriginals.com/

 

Site Goal(s): To sell their product and advertise it.

Target Audience: Adults who drink.

Navigation: Navigation is very neat. You first have two links you can either click. One leads to the shop, the other has background information and recipes.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The use is easy because you don’t have to bounce between to many links to get to what you want. The links also have descriptions so you have a basic idea about where it will lead.

Content: The website has information about the company and prices of products. They have it organized so if you want to read the backstory of the company you can.

Design/Layout: I like that the website uses both photos and video in their displays. The products are labeled so a consumer can easily find what they need to know. Target Audience: Artists, possible employers, and those who want to know more about the artist.

Content:The content is very interesting, including photos of the work that the artist has made. They basically summarize what they have done and talk about who they are.

Design/Layout: Although once more a simple layout, the most unique design to this website is that the creator uses the artist’s initials as a background element that appears frequently along with the artist’s name.
