#1 : Julie Bonnemoy Portfolio

Site Goal(s): The site’s goal is to display and advertise Julie’s design abilities as well as show her past projects and employers. The portfolio is to be shown to new clients and to gain more work.

Target Audience: The site targets potential clients such as businesses, brands, photographers, fashion designers, etc. People who needs things like a site design, packaging, and other graphic elements.
The site also caters to younger clients like 30s and under. The colors and design is simple and modern.

Navigation: The navigation is clean and simple. The home page is scrolling and has many images. The projects Julie has done is another menu that covers the home page. The about me is a small separate page. The site has lots of info and picture examples but stays simplistic but graphically pleasing.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is legible, and simple in design. The menus are responsive and are easy to find. Text and images neat and legible even with the added animations.

Content: The site features Julie’s various projects. The images give a good sneak peek at what she has done and is capable of. The site also has various animations and graphics to make it visually appealing and fun to navigate. The site would seem boring and too simple without those extra elements.

Design/Layout: The home page brings your attention with the colorful 3D blob animations. The site uses san serif text at larger sizes, and colorful imagery. The text and backgrounds are usually black, white, or a flat color. The content is contained to 2 pages, and 1 menu to make it fast to navigate.

#3 : Adult Swim Singles 2017

Site Goal(s): The site’s goal is to entertain and inform the audience of 2017’s top singles picked by Adult Swim. The site shows 40 singles, the title, and artist.

Target Audience: Adult Swim and their music choices caters to younger audiences like 30 and under. Most of the singles were electronic, pop, and R&B genres. The graphics also have a modern and electronic feel to them further catering to younger audiences.

Navigation: The singles are navigated via scrolling or clicking on the next line. Hovering over the bottom brings up a menu of lines that shows the number, song, and artist so it’s easy to skip and move around. The tip bar has links to other Adult Swim activities and products.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is eye catching and easy to navigate, it has multiple ways to move and skip through the 40 singles. The text is large and easy to read. The site also brings up a music player in the bottom right corner to show how far you are in and the pause button for easy use.

Content: The site shows 40 different songs and their artists. From 2017 The site is simply a music player but is an engaging design and pleasing to the eye. The site also has external links to other Adult Swim related things.

Design/Layout: The design is primarily black and dark colors, light text, and occasional pops of bright colors. It’s legible and easy on the eyes. The home page also has 3D animations that are floating shapes, and they respond to your mouse clicks and drags.

#5 : In My World

Site Goal(s): The site’s goal is to raise awareness and help for those suffering from mental illnesses, human rights violations, and marginalized communities across the world. Especially those with few other options to help them.

Target Audience: The site targets those who are charitable and seek to help others. The site shows the devastation people must face, and presents solutions to inspire readers to take action and help.

Navigation: The site starts with a message to proceed, then comes to the home page full of photos with different stories. The different photos bring up different stories, and you scroll through to finish them. It responds fast and cleanly.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is easy to navigate, primarily with scrolling. The site also has graphics to show how far you are into a story, and can skin ahead or back.

Content: The site shares stories about mental illness, poverty, marginalized communities, overcrowded prisons, war, and faith. The site’s content is primarily written stories, but has many photographs and video clips to explain more about their stories. This site also contains links to take action, and information about the project and it’s launchers.

Design/Layout: The site uses dismal black and white colors, and serif fonts. The site is saddening, but also visually appealing and professional. There is also some animation on the text and graphics to make it look more interesting. The site focuses most on showing the photographs of those suffering and their stories.

#7 : KIKK Festival

Site Goal(s): The site’s goal is to show information about the Kikk Festival, a festival of digital and creative cultures. The site also aims to sell tickets and bring in more guests and customers to help the festival grow.

Target Audience: This site targets designers, artists, developers, speakers, and other creative minds who would be interested in attending this festival. The festival focuses mainly on design and technology but also has interest in sciences and other areas of research.

Navigation: The pages are long scrolling pages, and the top has various links to more information about the festival, its schedule, and other things.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): the site has many graphics and elements, but it’s still easy to use and load. The navigation is clear and simple so it’s hard to get lost.

Content: The site has a lot of content. Ranging from festival information, guests, schedules, the staff, venues, and information on tickets and sponsors. It’s easy to find these different things and find what you are interested in.

Design/Layout: The site has a very modern and technological feel, and is primarily hues of blue and purple. The site also used effects like glitching on the photos to also give it that modern tech feel. The site uses long scrollable pages with different links at the top.

#9 : Falter Inferno

Site Goal(s): The site’s goal is to raise awareness of all the troubles plaguing our mind, society, and world, and how we as humans need to be aware and have to change to preserve it.
The site gives a message of all we ignore and suffer from, and provides a subscription as a solution with Falter. A subscription to articles and other resources to aid readers in becoming more well-rounded and aware.

Target Audience: The audience likely targets adults around 30-50s. But it relates to anyone who is unhappy and becoming aware of today’s problems. It also targets those interesting in reading and more knowledge.

Navigation: The site is primarily animations that you scroll through by interacting, such as dragging down each page.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site’s navigation is more interactive which is less common, but it is simple and gives instructions just in case. Despite having many effects like large animations, music, and more it is still easy to navigate.

Content: Each part has a different animation, text, and audio. And it is quite immersive and well made. Each part shows different problems like the addiction to technology, deforestation, and violence.

Design/Layout: The layout seems to be one large page, with a transition between each part or animation. The site ends with a peaceful animation and information about the subscription, as well as a button to visit the Falter page.

#2 : Zoomanity

Site Goal(s): Zoomanity shows illustration, design, and culture with this interesting project. The animals have human stories and history. The site aims to entertain and inform about past events and the fictional tales of these animals.

Target Audience: The site targets different countries including Russia and the US. The site shows design and artistic skills as well as writing. It caters to those fond of culture and fiction, as well as those interested in graphic and web design, as well as art. Zoomanity can prove useful as a portfolio piece for the designers to show to potential clients.
The site also has an online store featuring products of the characters, which also targets those who would be interested in this story.

Navigation: The site’s main navigation is scrolling, and the menus are responsive as the progress through the stories and characters the farther down you go. You can also skip characters down the list. The site also features sharing and a shop button to navigate to those pages.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is very clean and simple. It’s easy to scroll through and read the different quotes and navigate the different characters. The share and shop buttons are also easy to find. Even with the different animations and layers, the site runs fast and easy.
The site does, however, have issues with small screens and will warn you to find a larger device to view it properly.

Content: The content is simple and clean. Each character has an illustration and some historical photos. The text is legible and broken up into small parts like quotes.

Design/Layout: The site’s design is simple, with very few colors and large text and titles. The different parts respond to your scrolling and move around accordingly.

#4 : Coulee Creative

Site Goal(s): The site’s goal is to advertise the potential and skills of the digital agency Coulee Creative. The homepage goes on to say they care for their clients, and also support long distance relations. The site seeks to find new clients for this agency.

Target Audience: The site targets younger audiences around 30s and under. The site has a fun yet professional atmosphere that emulates well with younger people so they feel more welcome and unintimidated. The site also has various animations, art, and photos that give it a friendly and modern feel.

Navigation: The home page is a longer scrollable page, and has basic information about the agency’s goals and beliefs when it comes to their clients. The middle button on the home page also brings up more pages and further information about the agency and the work they do.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is graphically appealing but also fast and easy to navigate. The menus may be a bit less unconventional but it’s creative. The middle button brings up many other options and information. The colors and text are appealing but also easy on the eyes, text isn’t full black and the whites are a bit muted.

Content: Most of the content is under the menu, and is organized. They also have their own graphics to make them stand out amongst the other listed items.

Design/Layout: The design is eye catching, but not overwhelming. The colors are harmonious and easy to look at. The text is big and legible, the images are crisp and easy to navigate through.

#6 : Victoria Spicer Portfolio

Site Goal(s): This site’s goal is to be a portfolio of Victoria’s past works and to show her skills and designs to new clients.

Target Audience: This site targets clients who need designers for their sets and other elements. People who need her skills for stage performances, photo shoots, product photography, and more.

Navigation: The site’s main navigation is scrolling, and the buttons at the top bring up different pages to show more information about her past projects and more about herself.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is very image heavy but it doesn’t lag or slow down. It is also still easy to read text that may be on top the images. The typefaces are big and legible fonts, and the colors don’t upset the eyes. The navigation is also easy and simple, with just a home page and 4 other tabs.

Content: The site contains many images of Victoria’s projects and designs, as well as information about how to contact her and her design beliefs. The Projects tab especially is full of information and images to give visitors a clear idea of her abilities as a designer.

Design/Layout: The site uses dark and light muted colors primarily, and is eye catching. The site is also very image heavy and decorated, but it isn’t overwhelming.

#8 : In Pieces

Site Goal(s): This site aims to raise awareness and action to help 30 different animal species that are in danger of extinction. The site also interests those interesting in art and animation, since each animal is created out of the same 30 shapes or pieces.

Target Audience: It targets those passionate for the environment and are charitable. The site aims to make the users feel saddened and also awed by the beauty of the graphics about these animals, so they care for them.

Navigation: The site contains 30 different animals, and to navigate you can scroll up or down or use the arrows. Each animal has more information accessible as well as resources, causes, and how the piece was made.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is clean, fast, and legible. The colors pop but don’t disturb your eyes, and the text and links are legible and easy to navigate. The site also contains sound which can be muted

Content: The site contains graphics of each endangered animal, as well as information about preservation causes and resources, the reasons for its endangerment, and other information people will need to know to help them.

Design/Layout: The design is very geometric and colorful. The animals are depicted in 30 pieces, and the different menus change in mood. When you click on the threats, the palettes change to darks and reds giving it a sense of foreboding.

#10 : Sons of Gallipoli

Site Goal(s): The site’s goal is to inform, with a documentary about two heros of war.

Target Audience: The site targets those who are interested in history, and those who fought in wars.

Navigation: The site’s main page starts with a graphic and a button to begin the story at chapter one. It’s easy to follow and continue with. The site also has the chapters available at the top left menu. The site will also resume from where you left off. And within a chapter’s video you can pause and change the chapter.

Usability Issues (Ease of Use): The site is video heavy, but it’s easy to navigate. The site contains subtitles, languages, volume control, and easy chapter selecting as well as forwarding and rewinding. The menus also give access to other information about this story.

Content: The site’s contact is multiple video clips about the Gallipoli sons, as well as photos, and a map to help the reader understand the geography.

Design/Layout: The design is very professional and clean. With primarily black and golden hues. Each video is clean, the graphics are high quality, and the animations and menus are responsive and simplistic in design but still fun to look at.
