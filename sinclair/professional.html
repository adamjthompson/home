Currently, I manage the web site for the Ohio Association of Two-Year Colleges (OATYC). I primarily handle basic content updates a few times per year, but recently I moved the site to a new server which required a re-design from scratch. It runs on a new installation of WordPress now, so content updates are relatively easy to manage.

I kept the design very simple overall since the site is rather light on content, but I focused on keeping it clean, minimal, and responsive. The original site was not formatted well for mobile devices, so I made that a priority with the re-design.

I served on the planning committee for the 2018 Ohio Association of Two-Year Colleges annual conference, held at Sinclair Community College.

As the graphic designer for the event, I developed the logo, awards luncheon booklet, name tags, notepad, and multi-page program. Additionally, I assisted with creating content for and managing the web site used for the event.

The grayscale logos are some of the variations created before deciding on the final look, shown in color. Mosaic tiles provided the inspiration for the segmentation within the lettering. I also created a version that utilizes a recolored image for the texture.

In 2013, I was on the Fall Faculty Professional Development Day Committee (that year titled the "Fall Learning Day"), and I volunteered myself to design a logo for the event. Traditionally, logos had been developed each year by non-designers, and the results were usually less than spectacular.

The theme in 2013 was "Beyond the Classroom: Educating for Life". I designed the header image below to be intentionally read in two directions: The bold text can be read vertically stacked as "Beyond Educating" while the colored text can be read horizontally in its two parts.

The pattern behind the header was intended to evoke feelings of brain cells connecting as well as people networking together.

For a period of time, I worked with the media team at Far Hills Community Church to create various slides that would be projected before the weekly services.

These were mostly quick-turnaround Photoshop graphics, designed from stock assets found online. I developed the background textures and laid out the text. The logos were provided by another designer within the church.
